1 Introduction
Generalized linear models include as special cases, linear regression and analysis-
of-variance models, logit and probit models for quantal responses, log linear
models and multinomial response models for counts and some commonly used
models for survival data.
The second-order properties of the parameter estimates are insensitive to
the assumed distributional form: the second-order properties depend mainly on
the assumed variance-to-mean relationship and on uncorrelatedness or indepen-
dence.

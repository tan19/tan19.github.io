2.1 Vector Space
Deﬁnition 2.1.1 A vector space over a ﬁeld F is a nonempty set V together with the operations of
addition V × V → V and scalar multiplication F × V → V satisfying the following eight properties:
(-) Additive axioms. For every u, v, w ∈ V , we have
(1) u + v = v + u
(2) (u + v) + w = u + (v + w)
(3) 0 + u = u + 0 = u, where 0 ∈ V is unique for all u ∈ V
(4) (−u) + u = u + (−u) = 0, where −u ∈ V is unique for every u ∈ V
(-) Multiplicative axioms. For every u ∈ V and scalars a, b ∈ F, we have
(1) 1x = x
(2) (ab)x = a(bx)
(-) Distributive axioms. For every u, v ∈ V and scalars a, b ∈ F, we have
(1) a(u+v) = au+ av
(2) (a+b)u= au+ bu

There exist two separate branches of finance that require advanced quantitative techniques: derivatives pricing, and risk and portfolio management. One of the main differences is that they use different probabilities, namely the risk-neutral probability (or arbitrage-pricing probability), denoted by “Q”, and the actual (or actuarial) probability, denoted by “P”. (from wikipedia, article on Mathematical Finance)

The Q world (Derivatives Pricing)

The goal of derivatives pricing is to determine the fair price of a given security in terms of more liquid securities whose price is determined by the law of supply and demand.

Goal “extrapolate the present”
Environment risk-neutral probability Q
Processes continuous-time martingales
Dimension low
Tools Ito calculus, PDEs
Challenges calibration
Business sell-side

  • A martingale does not reward risk, hence the name “risk-neutral”.

  • Securities are priced individually, and thus the problems in the Q world are low-dimensional in nature.

The P world (Risk and Portfolio Management)

Based on the P distribution, the buy-side community takes decisions on which securities to purchase in order to improve the prospective profit-and-loss profile of their positions considered as a portfolio.

Goal “model the future”
Environment real-world probability P
Processes discrete-time series
Dimension large
Tools multivariate statistics
Challenges estimation
Business buy-side
